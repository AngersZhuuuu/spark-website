Spark Community

Mailing Lists

Get help using Spark or contribute to the project on our mailing lists:

  • user@spark.incubator.apache.org is for usage questions, help, and announcements. (subscribe) (archives)
  • dev@spark.incubator.apache.org is for people who want to contribute code to Spark. (subscribe) (archives)

Events and Meetups

Conferences:

  • Spark Summit: In December 2013, the community held the first Spark Summit, with 30 talks from organizations using Apache Spark. Visit the Summit archive page for slides and videos of all the talks. Spark Summit 2014 is also coming up.

Meetups:

  • Bay Area Spark Meetup: This group has been running monthly events since January 2012 in the San Francisco area. The meetup page also contains an archive of past meetups, including videos and slides on most of the recent pages.
  • Seattle Spark Meetup: Newly formed group in Seattle.

If you'd like your meetup added, email user@spark.incubator.apache.org.

Issue Tracker

The project tracks bugs and new features on JIRA. If you'd like, you can also subscribe to the spark-issues group to receive emails about new or updated issues.

Project History

Spark started as a research project at the UC Berkeley AMPLab in 2009, and was open sourced in early 2010. Many of the ideas behind the system are presented in various research papers. The AMPLab continues to perform research on both improving Spark and on systems built on top, such as Shark, GraphX, and Spark Streaming.

After being released, Spark grew a developer community on GitHub and entered Apache in 2013 as its permanent home. A wide range of contributors now develop the project (over 120 developers from 25 companies).
